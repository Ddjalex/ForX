The Keno Profit Control Margin Range System is a backend management tool used by casino operators and lottery organizations to regulate the profitability of a Keno game. It is not a "winning strategy" for players; rather, it is a set of mathematical parameters and software controls that ensure the "house" maintains a consistent profit margin within a specific target range.Here is a breakdown of how these systems typically function and why they are used.1. The "Margin Range" (Target Hold)In the gambling industry, the margin (often called the "hold percentage" or "house edge") is the portion of total wagers that the operator expects to keep after all prizes are paid out.1The Target: For Keno, this margin is notoriously high, usually ranging from 20% to 35%.The Range: Because Keno results are random, the actual profit fluctuates daily.2 A "Margin Range System" allows operators to define an acceptable "band" of variance (e.g., 22% to 28%). If the actual payout falls outside this range, the system alerts management to investigate for potential fraud, technical errors, or "hot" streaks that might threaten the house's liquidity.2. Profit Control MechanismsThe "Profit Control" aspect refers to the specific software triggers used to keep the house's liability in check. These mechanisms include:Liability Limits: The system sets a maximum "payout cap" for any single game or draw. For example, if too many players choose the same "hot" numbers and the total payout exceeds a certain limit (e.g., $100,000 for one draw), the system may trigger a Prorating mechanism.Prorating & Pari-Mutuel Payouts: If the liability limit is reached, the system automatically switches from fixed payouts to a "pari-mutuel" model. This means the total prize pool is divided among the winners, ensuring the house never pays out more than it can afford or more than its "profit control" settings allow.Dynamic Payout Tables: Advanced systems can adjust the payout odds for future games if the actual hold percentage is consistently falling below the target margin.3.
